## Product overview

The MRDVS M4-G is an RGB-D camera based on iToF technology. Designed for close-range perception on embodied robots, it provides a GMSL2 interface. The camera operates from 0.05-1m with distance accuracy up to 4mm. It synchronously outputs depth data and 1080P RGB images at up to 30fps for close-range perception, tabletop object grasping, and dexterous manipulation.

## Core technology and applications

- Depth sensing: Uses ToF technology to output close-range depth data.

- Low-light operation: Uses active illumination to output depth data at night or in low light.

- Fewer data gaps: Active illumination reduces missing data and improves depth completeness.

- Multiple output formats: Synchronously outputs depth and RGB images with RGB alignment.

- Data transmission: Provides a GMSL2 interface for longer transmission distances and interference-resistant connections.

**Primary applications:**

The camera mounts on a humanoid robot wrist (VLA) for tabletop object grasping and dexterous industrial operations.

- Tabletop object grasping: Humanoid robots autonomously identify and grasp objects on a tabletop for home service, office assistance, and similar applications.

- Dexterous manipulation: High-precision vision guides humanoid robots through part identification, grasping, and assembly in industrial tasks such as random bin picking, precision assembly, and machine tending.

To simulate a GPIO trigger signal on MFP0 inside the 96717, see `trig.c`.

### Stream-start command received

i2ctransfer -f -y 4 w3@0x11 0x20 0x00 1

For 0x2000: 0 stops the stream; 1 starts the stream.

0x11 is the FPGA address.
