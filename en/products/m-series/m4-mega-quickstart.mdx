This guide covers package inspection, power and network setup, and the first data stream from an M4 Mega camera.

<Warning>
Disconnect power before installing or connecting cables. Do not obstruct or touch the lens.
</Warning>

  <Step title="Configure the computer network">
    The M4 Mega factory-default IP address is `192.168.100.82`. Set the computer's Ethernet adapter to the same subnet, for example `192.168.100.100`, with subnet mask `255.255.0.0`.

    In Windows, open **Control Panel** → **Network and Internet** → **Network and Sharing Center** → **Change adapter settings** → **Ethernet** → **Internet Protocol Version 4 (TCP/IPv4)**.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/products/m-series-m4-mega-quickstart-windows-ipv4-network-settings.avif" alt="Windows IPv4 address settings" />
    </Frame>

    If the software cannot discover the camera, check whether the local firewall is blocking the connection.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Install LxCameraViewer">
    LxCameraViewer displays images, configures camera parameters, and selects built-in application algorithms. Windows provides the graphical host application; on Linux, use the examples and RViz to view data.

    - [CameraSDK GitHub repository](https://github.com/Lanxin-MRDVS/CameraSDK)
    - [Software downloads](/en/resources/downloads/software-and-sdk)
  </Step>

## Usage precautions

- Do not drop the camera, subject it to impact, or apply other mechanical stress.
- Do not modify internal camera files or disassemble the device; doing so may cause permanent damage.
- The enclosure may become warm during long operation. The camera uses passive cooling, so increase the contact area between the camera and the metal mounting plate when installing it.
- Dirt or fingerprints on the lens can reduce image quality.
- If the camera is not detected, verify the **24 VDC / 2 A** supply and check the Ethernet cable.

## Safety

<Warning>
Do not power on a visibly damaged device. Do not disassemble, modify, or repair the camera yourself, and do not point the lens directly at the sun.
</Warning>
