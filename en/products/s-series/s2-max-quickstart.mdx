The camera uses a DC power supply. For descriptions of the DC cable signals, see section 3.7, Product interface, in the [S2 Max camera user guide](/en/products/s-series/s2-max-user-guide "S2 Max camera user guide").

<Step title="Configure the IP address">

1.  The camera's factory-default IP address is 192.168.100.82. Set the computer to the 192.168.100.XXX subnet.

2. The camera and computer must be on the same subnet before LxCameraViewer can open the camera.

3. Disable the computer's network firewall before connecting the camera.

</Step>

<Step title="Configure the computer network">

Open **Control Panel** and select **Network and Internet** → **Network and Sharing Center** → **Change adapter settings** → **Ethernet** → **Internet Protocol Version 4 (TCP/IPv4)**. In the **Internet Protocol Version 4 (TCP/IPv4) Properties** dialog, select **Use the following IP address**, enter an address on the camera's 192.168.100.XXX subnet, and set the subnet mask to 255.255.0.0.

<Step title="Download LxCameraViewer to view camera output">

1.  Use LxCameraViewer to view and configure camera image parameters.

2. Use LxCameraViewer to configure the camera's built-in obstacle-avoidance, pallet, navigation, and picking algorithms.

3. Select and download LxCameraViewer for your system and version. A GUI is available for Windows but not Linux. On ROS, run an example and view images in rviz.

- GitHub: [Lanxin-MRDVS/CameraSDK](https://github.com/Lanxin-MRDVS/CameraSDK)

- Software: [Download software](https://zcnt5g3o2989.feishu.cn/wiki/Q8IYwGiGaiIvIyktiavczymanOe?from=from_copylink "Download software")

</Step>

<Step title="Start LxCameraViewer">

1.  Double-click the LxCameraViewer installer. After installation, configure the computer's IP address and disable its network firewall.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/legacy/736c0f93-8aab-442c-81ec-57f1eaad7e71/f82872e5-0f79-4598-91d2-ee88998420d4.avif" alt="LxCameraViewer interface" />
</Frame>

2. Start LxCameraViewer and check whether the camera ID appears in the device list on the left.

- If nothing appears, check whether the camera indicator flashes every 2 seconds.

- If it flashes normally, troubleshoot the network connection.

- If it remains on without flashing, contact MRDVS technical support.

- If it is off, verify that the power supply meets the 24V 2A requirement.

### Camera usage precautions

**Operate the device as described in this guide. Incorrect operation can damage internal components.**

1.  Do not drop the camera or subject it to mechanical stress.

2.  Do not modify the camera's internal files. Doing so can cause permanent damage.

3.  The camera may become warm during extended use, but this does not affect imaging or ranging accuracy. The camera uses passive cooling. Increase the contact area between the camera and its metal mounting plate to improve heat dissipation.

4.  Do not touch the lens. Fingerprints can affect image quality.

5.  If the camera is not detected, verify that the power cable supplies 24V / 2A and that the Ethernet cable is not damaged.

**Camera safety and handling:**

1.  Do not power on the product if you observe external damage.

2.  Do not open any part of the product. Unauthorized disassembly voids repair service.

3.  Be aware of invisible laser radiation. Do not point the camera directly toward the sun.

4.  To maintain compliance and safety, do not modify or repair the product.
