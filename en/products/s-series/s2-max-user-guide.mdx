## 1. Product overview

This document describes the S2 Max camera developed by Zhejiang MRDVS Technology Co., Ltd., including its specifications, features, and operating instructions.

The S2 Max is an MRDVS ToF camera with a Gigabit Ethernet communication interface and 24V DC power input.

- Integrated 1 TOPS compute capacity; requires no external CPU

### 1.2 Terms of use

**Observe the following requirements during use:**

- Do not remove labels from the product.

- Do not disassemble the product. Doing so can damage it.

- Do not use the product in direct sunlight above 100KLUX.

- Clean the enclosure with a soft lint-free cloth dampened with a small amount of purified water or alcohol.

- Retain the original packaging and use it for transport when possible.

## 2. Product specifications

### 2.1 Ranging principle

The S2 Max measures distance using time of flight (ToF): it measures the time between emitting light and receiving the reflection from an object.

The S2 Max uses direct measurement for depth imaging, generating high-quality depth data with minimal compute resources and building depth scenes at a high frame rate. Like LiDAR, ToF cameras are divided into pulsed ToF (P\_ToF) and continuous-wave ToF (CW\_ToF). P\_ToF emits light pulses, usually invisible, and calculates distance from their round-trip time. CW\_ToF emits modulated continuous light and calculates flight time from the phase difference between transmitted and returned light.

### 3.3 Detection range

Figure 3.1 shows the S2 Max detection region. The horizontal detection angle is 81°, and the vertical angle is 61°. Detection distance depends on optical power. Higher optical power and target reflectivity increase the near blind zone and reduce the far blind zone. Lower optical power and target reflectivity reduce the near blind zone and increase the far blind zone. For targets with 10%~90% reflectivity, the S2 Max range is 0.2~5m. Beyond 5m, low reflected-signal strength and ambient-light interference cause substantial ranging errors.

- The world coordinate system is the 3D point-cloud coordinate system.

## 4. Accuracy and precision

### 4.1 Accuracy

Calibration removes a fixed offset between sensor measurements and actual values by establishing a mapping between them. For the S2 Max, mount the camera on a rail facing a white wall and map each pixel's measurement to the distance between the camera and the imaged wall region. Correcting measurements with this mapping provides more accurate distance values, as shown in Figure 4.1.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/legacy/1bf79e92-93f8-4dae-9b95-9853490d9888/e242df58-7178-48a9-a801-9f0513727e69.avif" alt="Calibration setup" />
</Frame>

Figure 4.1. Calibration

The S2 Max uses active ranging. Installation and other factors can introduce multipath interference and distort accuracy.

This effect is especially pronounced when measuring a low-reflectivity target beside a highly reflective object. The ideal measurement follows the blue path, but light can reflect from the wall to the target and back to the camera (green path). Illumination can also reflect directly from the nearby high-reflectivity object into the camera (red path). Optical imperfections create glare that interferes with ranging, as shown in Figure 4.2.

Figure 4.2. Camera installation

### 4.2 Precision

Precision usually means single-point variation (1-σ). As an optoelectronic measurement device, the S2 Max is affected by AD conversion, signal-to-noise ratio, system noise, and other factors. Figure 4.3 shows the relationship between precision and signal strength.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/legacy/4bdfd3a0-f5b8-4136-a633-3990ef7e3408/636c60f8-d5d0-41b0-849f-9dacb9ad6bc0.avif" alt="Precision and signal strength" />
</Frame>

Figure 4.3. Precision and signal strength

The S2 Max converts optoelectronic analog signals into digital data. ADC quantizes the analog signal at its minimum resolution and introduces noise. For example, a 1.1V signal may be read as 1V, an error of 10%; 10.1V may be read as 10V, an error of 1%; and 100.1V may be read as 100V. AD conversion therefore introduces proportionally more noise and error at low signal strength. A ToF camera's signal-to-noise ratio is typically related to integration time t by SNR∝1/√t, so increasing integration time raises signal strength.

## 5. Multi-camera interference

### 5.1 Parallel interference

When two cameras are mounted in parallel, each camera receives light returned from both itself and the other camera, interfering with depth data. Parallel interference is the increased detection error caused when two S2 Max cameras point in the same direction.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/legacy/a83df49c-2acc-4fde-98b2-f7c47bb80a10/5ab113bc-bb79-46fe-8695-fb9fae482ad0.avif" alt="Parallel camera interference" />
</Frame>

To avoid parallel interference, install the cameras at an angle to the horizontal plane. If they must be installed at 0°, contact MRDVS for a custom configuration.

### 6.2 SDK output order

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/legacy/ae0a3b9d-5f56-424e-a3d0-559042c29d3c/fb9ad6ad-08cc-4bfa-843a-12ca2083d122.avif" alt="SDK output order" />
</Frame>

Figure 6.1. SDK output order

The SDK outputs depth and intensity data one frame at a time as one-dimensional arrays at the configured resolution. The top-left pixel is the origin, and output proceeds from left to right and top to bottom. Figure 6.1 shows x-axis output followed by y-axis output:

(x,y):(0,0)(0,1)(0,2)(0,…)(1,0)(1,1)(1,2)(1,…)(2,…)(3,…)(…,…).

Each depth pixel is a 16-bit unsigned short that represents the distance from the measured object to the camera plane.

Each intensity pixel is a 16-bit unsigned short that represents the returned signal intensity.

### 6.3 IP address

The S2 Max factory-default IP address is 192.168.100.82. Use the MRDVS demo tool to change the IP address or enable DHCP.

### 6.4 Demo software

### 6.4.1 IP configuration

Connect the power cable, then connect the camera to the computer with an Ethernet cable. Because the factory-default camera IP is 192.168.100.82, configure the computer to 192.168.100.x, excluding 100.82, with subnet mask 255.255.255.0. Open **Settings** → **Ethernet** → **Change adapter options**, right-click **Ethernet**, and select **Internet Protocol Version 4 (TCP/IPv4)**. For example, set the IP address to 192.168.100.20 and the subnet mask to 255.255.255.0.

### 6.4.2 Open the software

Download and install the software, then double-click the LxCameraViwer demo icon. The host software opens, searches for cameras, opens the camera, and starts acquisition automatically when one camera is connected.

**Note: On first launch, some systems require you to grant firewall access.**

The software provides basic functions, image selection, camera configuration, and camera information. Camera information includes the camera IP, ID, MAC address, serial number, and software and algorithm versions. Select **Depth**, **Intensity**, **RGB**, or **Point cloud** to display the corresponding image.

### 6.4.3 Save images

Click **Save** to save the selected images to the `./data` folder in the software installation directory. The terminal also prints the path, for example: `save path= ./data/camera ID`.

In the saved files, `_a` is the intensity image, `_d` is the depth image, `_r` is the RGB (MONO) image, and `.pcd` is the point cloud. Depth and intensity images use PGM, RGB images use PNG, and point clouds use PCD.

Enter the required IP address, such as 192.168.10.199. Enter subnet mask 255.255.255.0 and gateway 192.168.10.1. The gateway must be on the same subnet as the required IP and normally ends in 1. Confirm the change, then power-cycle the camera.

### 8.2 Correct operation

The S2 Max camera uses a 24V DC supply. Connect the power cable before applying power, and do not reverse the positive and negative terminals. Reverse polarity can damage the product.

### 8.3 Operating temperature

Keep the ambient temperature within the range in the product specifications (Table 2-1). Mount the product on a thermally conductive component such as a metal bracket to improve heat dissipation and achieve optimal performance.
