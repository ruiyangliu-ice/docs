## Product overview

The S10 Series consists of industrial dToF RGB-D cameras, including S10, S10 Pro, and Lite versions. The cameras synchronize RGB and depth data and can output depth maps, IR amplitude images, RGB images, point clouds, and obstacle-avoidance results.

The standard S10 measures from 0.3–8 m and the S10 Pro from 0.3–17 m. Supported models provide Ethernet, two digital inputs, and two digital outputs. Configure up to four obstacle-avoidance zones and output stop, slowdown, or safe-state signals.

## Features

- **Medium- and long-range dToF perception:** Suitable for indoor and outdoor mobile applications such as autonomous vehicles, autonomous forklifts, and drones.
- **Synchronized multimodal output:** Provides depth, RGB, IR amplitude, and point-cloud data. The Lite version does not provide RGB data.
- **On-device obstacle avoidance:** Receive point clouds over Ethernet or use the built-in algorithm to output control signals through I/O.
- **Multi-camera operation and bright-light performance:** Multiple cameras can work together. With a 100 kLux environment and a 90% reflective target, S10 reaches 8 m and S10 Pro reaches 17 m.

#### Common installation interference

- **Field-of-view obstruction:** Frames, ribs, small openings, or off-center mounting can create fixed blind spots or false near-field targets.
- **Near-field reflections and multipath:** Confined cavities, bright metal, or mirrors can distort distance or cause local saturation.
- **Optical-window problems:** Ordinary glass, acrylic, dirt, or condensation can introduce artifacts, attenuate signals, or lower the signal-to-noise ratio. Validate transmission and ranging performance before adding a window.
- **Mounting stress and heat:** Over-tightening can shift the optical axis; provide a heat path in enclosed installations to avoid temperature-related drift.

## First connection

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect power and network">
    Power the camera with 24 VDC and connect its communication interface to the computer with a Cat 6 Ethernet cable. A slowly flashing blue indicator indicates normal power.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure the network">
    The camera's factory-default IP address is `192.168.100.82`. Set the computer to the same subnet and make sure the firewall does not block camera communication.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify image output">
    Open the camera in the host application and confirm that the image is clear and stable. Configure basic parameters and application algorithms as needed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## LxCameraViewer interface

LxCameraViewer displays depth, IR amplitude, point-cloud, and RGB images and reads or configures basic camera and algorithm parameters.

## Safety and troubleshooting

### Laser safety

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/products/s-series-s10-overview-laser-safety-class.avif" alt="S10 Series laser safety label" />
</Frame>

Keep the safety label complete and legible, and follow the safety requirements in the label and product materials.

### Troubleshooting

| Problem | Suggested checks |
| --- | --- |
| No data after opening the camera | Check the firewall, confirm that the computer and camera share a subnet, and check the switch and Ethernet cable. |
| The host application detects multiple IP addresses | Resolve duplicate LAN IP addresses and select the correct device. |
| White objects have poor edge accuracy | Adjust glare-suppression parameters and recheck black-target performance. |
| Reflective columns are overexposed | Try the glare-suppression algorithm and validate it in the actual scene. |
| No image or an abnormal depth-image size | Update the host application to `Lanxin-MRDVS-1.3.66.0606` or later. |
| Exclusive-application permission fails | Make sure only one client is connected, or use a newer release with multi-user permissions. |
| Sparse or missing target data | Check the high-exposure parameter (typically 600–1200) and low-signal threshold (typically 10–30). |
