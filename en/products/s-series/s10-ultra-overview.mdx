## Product overview

The S10 Ultra is an industrial dToF RGB-D camera for medium- and long-range 3D perception indoors and outdoors. It synchronizes RGB and depth data and integrates an IMU with a data output rate of up to 200 Hz.

The camera works with SLAM algorithms such as FastLio and [FastLio2](https://github.com/Lanxin-MRDVS/MRDVS-S10Ultra_Mapping), supports software triggering, and is available as a MIPI module.

## Features and applications

- **Medium- and long-range dToF perception:** A 0.2–42 m measurement range supports environmental perception, localization, and navigation for mobile devices.
- **Synchronized data output:** Outputs depth, RGB, and IR amplitude data with RGB-D alignment.
- **Compact solid-state design:** Contains no moving parts and measures 106 × 69 × 43 mm.
- **IMU and SLAM development:** Provides 200 Hz IMU data for visual-inertial localization and mapping.

Typical applications include robotic lawn mowers, industrial quadruped robots, and multirotor drones.

## Installation requirements

<Warning>
Keep the illustrated clearance area free of obstructions. Components that enter this area can interfere with point clouds and cause noise, false detections, or incorrect stops.
</Warning>

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/products/s-series-s10-ultra-overview-recommended-installation.avif" alt="S10 Ultra recommended installation and clearance area" />
</Frame>

### Avoid these conditions

- **Clipped field of view:** Frames, ribs, openings, or off-center mounting can create blind spots and false near-field targets.
- **Near-field reflections and multipath:** Confined spaces, polished metal, and mirrors can distort distance or saturate local areas.
- **Optical interference from windows:** Ordinary glass, acrylic, dust, and condensation can cause artifacts, signal attenuation, or a lower signal-to-noise ratio. Validate the installation before adding a protective window.
- **Mounting stress or poor heat dissipation:** Over-tightening can affect the optical axis; provide a heat path in enclosed spaces to maintain ranging stability.

## First connection

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect the device">
    Power the camera with a 12–27 VDC / 3 A supply and connect it to the computer with a Cat 6 or better Ethernet cable. A slowly flashing blue indicator indicates normal power.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure the network">
    The camera's factory-default IP address is `192.168.100.82`. Set the computer to the same subnet and make sure the firewall does not block device communication.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify the stream">
    Open the camera in the host application and confirm that the image is clear and the stream is stable. Configure image, algorithm, and other device parameters as needed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Laser safety

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/products/s-series-s10-ultra-overview-laser-safety-class.avif" alt="S10 Ultra laser safety label" />
</Frame>

Keep the safety label complete and legible, and follow the safety requirements in the label and product materials.

### Troubleshooting

| Problem | Suggested checks |
| --- | --- |
| The camera does not respond when opened | Check whether the firewall is blocking communication and confirm that the computer and camera are on the same subnet. |
| The host application detects multiple IP addresses | Resolve LAN IP conflicts and select the correct device address. |
| The stream is unstable | Use a Gigabit Ethernet cable; Fast Ethernet may make initial data transfer unstable. |
| The software does not start correctly | Check permissions for the installation directory. |
