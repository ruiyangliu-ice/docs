## The importance of unmanned forklift pallet recognition

As more and more companies imitate industry giants, the application of automated equipment has gradually expanded from sorting to handling. As the core equipment in warehousing automation, unmanned forklifts (AGVs) undertake the important task of pallet handling. However, there is often uncertainty about the position and angle of pallets in warehousing, usually due to human intervention or placement errors. This poses a severe challenge to the navigation accuracy of unmanned forklifts, especially in the context of the growing demand for fast and precise operations. The accuracy of pallet recognition directly affects the efficiency and safety of the entire warehousing system. Therefore, the most accurate pallet visual recognition technology has become the key to ensuring the efficient and accurate operation of unmanned forklifts.

Automated pallet recognition uses vision sensors and image processing to provide precise pallet position and pose data in real time. This improves unmanned forklift navigation and handling efficiency, even when pallets have shifted or are placed at irregular angles.

## Challenges and solutions encountered in practical applications of pallet recognition

Although pallet recognition technology has made significant progress, unmanned forklifts still face a series of challenges in practical applications. Complexities in the warehousing environment, such as uneven lighting, pallet placement deviations, and multiple pallet specifications, will affect the accuracy of pallet identification. Traditional QR code or barcode recognition relies on clear labels and regular placement angles. Although it works well in structured environments, it is prone to failure in complex environments, thus affecting the accuracy of handling.

In contrast, pallet recognition technology based on 3D ToF (time of flight) cameras has demonstrated higher robustness and greater adaptability to complex pallet shapes and environmental changes. In addition, although 2D lidar is also used for pallet identification, due to its sensitivity to the complex structure of the bottom of the pallet, it often misjudges obstacles, affecting the smooth docking of the unmanned forklift and the pallet. Therefore, to address these challenges, pallet recognition solutions that combine 3D vision and multi-sensor fusion are becoming the mainstream choice in the industry.

## MRDVS pallet recognition system PalletPro: solving the problem of pallet recognition automation

MRDVS's PalletPro pallet recognition system is based on 3D ToF (time of flight) vision technology and is specially designed to meet the needs of pallet recognition in complex warehousing environments. The system combines a 3D camera with an intelligent algorithm to not only accurately identify the position and posture of the pallet, but also has excellent environmental adaptability, ensuring that unmanned forklifts can efficiently realize automated handling under complex conditions.

- **Integration and Efficiency Improvement**<br />By integrating the recognition algorithm into the camera, the PalletPro system can process depth data in real time, greatly improving computing efficiency and response speed. The system can provide real-time pallet recognition results at a processing speed of **10 frames per second (10FPS)**, ensuring that unmanned forklifts can quickly and accurately carry out pallet grabbing and handling operations.

- **Deployment and Intelligent Docking**<br />By deploying the 3D camera between the two fork arms of the forklift, the PalletPro system uses automatic calibration technology to determine the position and orientation of the pallet and the coordinate relationship between the forklift workshop, achieving fast and intelligent docking operations. The algorithm is based on point cloud features in depth data to identify the legs and crossbar structures of pallets. It supports the recognition requirements of standard two-legged pallets, four-legged pallets and multi-pallet stacking. It can also customize the recognition solution for legless or special-shaped pallets. The PalletPro system can adapt to more than 90% of pallet types on the market without collecting additional data for model training.

- **Ease of use and compatibility**<br />PalletPro is easy to deploy, and users can get started quickly through the introductory tutorial without algorithm development experience. The system is compatible with European standard pallets and is suitable for high-level storage, complex stacking and other scenarios. It provides real-time pallet identification results and can run stably in various warehousing environments, ensuring that unmanned forklifts can operate efficiently under diverse pallet conditions.

## Identification and docking

The MRDVS M series camera is installed between the two fork arms of the forklift to capture the depth information of the pallet and use the depth recognition algorithm to achieve accurate identification of the pallet.

When the forklift receives a task from the dispatching system, it moves to the docking point in front of the pallet, about 2 meters from the front edge of the pallet. At this position, the forklift performs preliminary positioning, and the 3D vision system will output pose information to help the forklift adjust the angle deviation. Then, the forklift continues to move forward to a position of 1.5 meters for precise positioning, and the 3D vision system outputs the pallet posture information again to help the forklift adjust the left and right deviations to ensure the accuracy of the docking process.

The solution supports two docking modes: **twice docking** and **real-time docking** to meet the needs of different application scenarios.

**1. Double docking mode**<br />Two docking modes are divided into three steps: remote positioning, near-end verification and blind fork retrieval:

**Remote positioning**: After receiving the dispatch task, the forklift moves to the docking point and enables the camera to identify the pallet data. Through the motion control system, the forklift can adjust the angle and lateral offset within the range of 1800mm to 2800mm from the pallet.

**Proximal verification**: When the fork tip is about 200mm away from the front edge of the pallet, obtain the camera identification data again to verify whether the docking accuracy meets the fork picking requirements. If the accuracy meets the requirements, the forklift will carry out the forking operation; if it does not meet the requirements, the forklift operation will be carried out after adjusting in place.

**2. Real-time docking mode**<br />The real-time docking mode achieves dynamic posture adjustment by continuously acquiring camera recognition data. When the forklift moves to about 200mm from the front edge of the pallet, the system makes fine adjustments and completes the forklift operation. This schema relies on the timestamp of the data for updates to avoid erroneous adjustments caused by delays.

## Communication method

In order to provide users with products that are easier to integrate, the solution supports TCP, UDP, CAN, 485, API and other interfaces, and can switch between recognition algorithms and obstacle avoidance algorithms to achieve efficient, accurate and universal pallet recognition automation.
