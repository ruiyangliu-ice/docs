In industrial restricted areas, automated lines, and warehouse work zones, an unauthorized entry can create an operational risk. This solution uses a 3D camera and human-recognition software to detect entries into a configured area and report events to a higher-level system.

## Solution overview

The system captures RGB-D data with an M Series 3D camera. Human-recognition software checks whether a person crosses a boundary within the configured ROI. Detection events can trigger an alarm or another action in the higher-level system.

## System components

### M4 Pro 3D depth camera

- Provides 3D information about people moving in the monitored space.
- Depth resolution: `640 × 480`; RGB resolution: `1920 × 1080`.
- Aligns RGB and depth data in both space and time.
- Can run supported algorithms on the device to reduce the need for an external industrial computer.

### Intelligent human-recognition software

- Configure a region of interest (ROI).
- Detect and report a person crossing the restricted boundary.

## Workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Configure the restricted area">
    Define the ROI from the site layout and camera field of view, excluding areas that are not part of the detection task.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Monitor continuously">
    The camera captures the restricted area while the software checks whether a person enters or crosses the configured boundary.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Report the event">
    When an event is detected, the system reports it to the higher-level system, which can trigger an alarm, stop operation, or take another configured action.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Application scenarios

- Automated production lines
- Warehouse and logistics areas
- Hazardous-material storage areas
- Workshops or laboratories with personnel exclusion requirements

## Performance notes

Original test records report single-frame false-positive and missed-detection rates under ideal laboratory conditions. The test used an open field of view, an unobstructed person within `5 m` of the camera, a movement speed below `3 m/s`, and good ambient lighting. Actual results depend on camera height, field of view, occlusion, posture, lighting, motion blur, and filter settings.

<Tip>
Adjusting the software filter window changes detection stability and response time. Use results from the target site as the acceptance basis.
</Tip>

</Warning>

## Deployment recommendations

- Keep the lens clean and check regularly for obstructions in the camera field of view.
- Validate the system on site before deployment in areas with heavy dust, strong reflections, or other extreme conditions.
- Revalidate the detection range and response chain after changing the camera position, detection area, or higher-level action logic.
