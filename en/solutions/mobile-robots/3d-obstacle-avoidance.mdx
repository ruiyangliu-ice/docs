**Mobile robot 3D visual obstacle avoidance**

As mobile robots achieve large-scale applications in warehousing, logistics, medical and health and other fields, how to ensure that mobile robots can achieve obstacle avoidance functions stably and reliably in complex environments has become an important technical challenge currently faced. In practical applications, the obstacle avoidance function is imperfect, which can easily lead to safety hazards such as collisions and accidents. Reliable obstacle avoidance requires the robot to have accurate environmental perception and understanding capabilities during movement.

**2D lidar has the following limitations in obstacle avoidance**

**· Lack of height information**: The scanning range of 2D lidar is usually limited to a plane, and it is easy to miss low or suspended obstacles.

**·Poor performance in complex scenes**: In complex or dynamic environments, it is difficult for 2D lidar to accurately identify and respond to diverse obstacle shapes and position changes.

## Solution Overview

As the first domestic company focusing on mobile robot vision technology, Meyer Micro Vision has launched the cost-effective RGB-D [Multi-modal industrial grade obstacle avoidance camera S series](https://www.mrdvs.cn/list-10.html "Multi-modal industrial-grade obstacle avoidance camera S series") has been used in batches in a variety of scenarios such as unmanned forklifts, stackers, latent trucks, bin trucks, industrial cleaning robots, and medical and service robots.

The S Series obstacle avoidance cameras capture 3D environmental data and RGB texture in real time, including low and overhanging obstacles. Their built-in obstacle avoidance algorithm combines RGB and depth images for semantic recognition and classification. It identifies each obstacle's position, size, and type, such as a person or an object of a particular shape. Compared with 2D LiDAR, the S Series helps mobile robots operate **more safely, reliably, and intelligently**.
