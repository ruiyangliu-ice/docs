SLAM (simultaneous localization and mapping) lets a robot build a map and estimate its pose in an unknown or changing environment. In the MRDVS top-view SLAM solution, an RGB-D camera is mounted on top of the vehicle and uses ceiling and other overhead features for localization.

## Solution characteristics

Compared with solutions that rely only on floor features, a top-view camera can reduce occlusion from floor cargo and people. It captures depth data above the vehicle and estimates the vehicle pose while building the map.

## Application examples

### Photovoltaic manufacturing workshop

The production equipment and transport robots at this site were commissioned in stages, so the environment and traffic changed continuously. The project mounted a top-view SLAM module on an integrated transport robot to maintain stable positioning at machine docking points. Project records state that the two workshops covered about `80,000 m²` and that more than `500` AGVs used the module during the recorded period.

### Automotive manufacturing workshop

This open workshop has fast-changing traffic from material carts, components, and personnel. The navigation sensor was mounted on top of a roller-conveyor transport AMR to localize engine-component transport.

  <img src="/images/solutions/mobile-robots-cases-slam-in-industrial-environments-case-sharing-3d-visual-slam-navigation-helps-mobile-robots-operate-04.avif" alt="Top-view localization on a roller-conveyor AMR in an automotive workshop" />
</Frame>

### Floor-stacked garment warehouse

The warehouse has more than `4,000` storage locations with narrow spacing and frequently changing inventory. The navigation module was installed on top of an AGV forklift for pallet transport localization.
