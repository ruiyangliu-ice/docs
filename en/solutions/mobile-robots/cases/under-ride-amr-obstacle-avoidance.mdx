The world's first notebook computer was launched by Toshiba in 1985, and the thin and light notebook Dynabook series was released in 1989. Entering the 21st century, the manufacturing industry faces the dual challenges of global competition and labor shortage. To this end, the Dynabook brand launched the intelligent transformation of its production base in China in 2019, introducing an advanced mobile robot system to improve logistics and production efficiency. **Project difficulties: workers and robots share passages, the scene is complex, and the robots need to accurately avoid various dynamic and static obstacles.**

Meyer Micro Vision's **S series 3D visual obstacle avoidance camera** provides the project's mobile robot with powerful environmental perception capabilities, enabling it to accurately avoid obstacles in a complex production environment and achieve high-precision docking with the material truck.

> **Main Feature Highlights**

1. **3D visual perception**: S series cameras simultaneously collect RGB and color depth data, allowing the robot to detect various dynamic and static obstacles. In particular, the detection of low obstacles is very sensitive, ensuring that the robot can successfully pass through narrow passages or areas with a lot of materials.

2. **Real-time dynamic obstacle avoidance**: S series cameras have the ability to respond efficiently to dynamic obstacles and can identify and avoid workers or other moving equipment in real time to ensure the safe operation of robots in the workshop.

3. **Adapt to complex environments**: Even in workshops with suboptimal lighting or complex environments, S series cameras can still provide stable sensing capabilities to ensure the normal operation of the robot.
