Among them, the focus is on the world's first purely visual humanoid robot - VersaBot (VersaBot) created by Myer Microvision and Vermyer VMR. The robot is designed to meet the intelligent needs of industry, logistics and other complex scenarios. It combines advanced 3D vision solutions and flexible motion control systems, and can efficiently handle tasks such as material grabbing, path navigation and real-time obstacle avoidance. The successful launch of Weibo humanoid robot is inseparable from the in-depth cooperation with Meyer Microvision. Based on Meyer Micro Vision's 3D vision technology, the Weibo robot realizes key functions such as high-precision grasping, autonomous navigation and intelligent obstacle avoidance, allowing it to operate flexibly in multiple scenarios and ensure safe and efficient task execution.

In the path planning and navigation process, Weibo humanoid robot relies on the Myer Micro Vision T series visual SLAM camera to ensure that it can achieve accurate autonomous navigation in complex and changeable environments. SLAM technology enables robots to have simultaneous positioning and mapping capabilities, allowing them to quickly adapt and complete tasks even in narrow spaces or dynamic areas.

 **Visual SLAM solution highlights**

**• Real-time positioning and mapping:** Through high-precision visual data, Weibo robots can build environment maps and plan paths in real time.

**• 30-meter sensing range:**  The visual SLAM solution supports a 30-meter sensing range, providing a strong guarantee for the robot's remote navigation in large warehouses and complex workshops.

**• Flexible response to dynamic scenes:**  Even in environments with dense personnel or complex equipment, it can respond quickly to ensure the stability and flexibility of robot path planning.

> When sharing the work space with workers and other equipment, the humanoid robot Weibo adopts an intelligent obstacle avoidance solution based on the S series RGB-D vision sensor. This solution provides the robot with comprehensive 3D environment perception and semantic recognition capabilities, ensuring that it can make intelligent decisions and avoid obstacles, ensuring the safety of collaborative operations.

 **Highlights of Intelligent Obstacle Avoidance Solution**

**• Semantic recognition and intelligent avoidance:** S series visual obstacle avoidance sensors combined with semantic recognition algorithms allow the robot to identify the categories and attributes of obstacles, and intelligently select detour or obstacle stopping strategies based on the actual situation to effectively avoid risks and avoid collision accidents.

**• Multi-modal perception capability:**  By fusing RGB and depth data, the robot can obtain wide-angle perception and accurately judge the surrounding environment even in scenes with complex lighting.

**• Anti-interference design:** The intelligent obstacle avoidance solution ensures that the robot can still operate stably in a multi-machine collaboration environment and avoids perception errors caused by signal interference.

> In terms of material grabbing and operation, the Weibo humanoid robot integrates the Meyer Micro Vision H series structured light camera, giving it high-precision operation capabilities in complex scenes. With the support of 3D point cloud data, H-series cameras allow robots to successfully complete grabbing tasks even when facing irregular objects or stacked items.

 **H series structured light camera solution highlights**

**• Sub-millimeter level accuracy:** H series cameras support sub-millimeter level 3D positioning to ensure accurate capture every time.

**• Complex object recognition:**  Even if the shape of the object is complex, the robot can accurately determine the shape and posture of the object, improving the success rate of grabbing. **Anti-light interference:** H series cameras can still work stably in complex lighting workshop environments, providing guarantee for high-intensity operations.
