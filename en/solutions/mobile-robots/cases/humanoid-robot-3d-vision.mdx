## Visual SLAM navigation

The robot uses a T Series visual SLAM camera for mapping and localization during path planning and navigation.

- **Mapping and localization:** Capture environmental features during operation to build a map and estimate the robot pose.
- **Dynamic-scene adaptation:** Provide environmental data for path planning as people, equipment, and aisles change.
- **Long-range perception:** The original project material lists a `30 m` sensing range. Confirm the usable range based on installation, scene features, and on-site validation.

## RGB-D obstacle perception

In workspaces shared with people and equipment, the robot uses an S Series RGB-D sensor to capture 3D environmental and texture data.

- **Obstacle recognition:** Use depth and RGB data to detect obstacle position, category, and attributes.
- **Avoidance inputs:** Feed perception results to higher-level policies for detouring, slowing, or stopping.
- **Multi-robot deployment:** Validate camera placement, overlapping fields of view, and on-site interference.

## 3D grasp perception

The robot uses an H Series structured-light camera to capture point clouds for object-shape and pose estimation during grasping.

- **3D localization:** Estimate the position and pose required for grasping.
- **Complex objects:** Support 3D perception of irregular or stacked objects.
- **Lighting conditions:** Actual performance depends on material, surface reflectivity, ambient light, and camera installation.
