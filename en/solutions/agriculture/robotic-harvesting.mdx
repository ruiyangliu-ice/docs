## Market background

China's agricultural picking robot market has shown a rapid growth trend in recent years. In 2023, China's picking robot market size will reach 61.78 million yuan, a year-on-year increase of 33.2%. It is expected that by 2030, the global picking robot market will reach 82.16 billion US dollars. This growth is driven by two main drivers:

**• Labor shortage**: The agricultural population is aging and labor costs are rising;

**•Technological breakthrough**: The maturity of AI, machine vision and sensor technology has laid the foundation for commercialization.

## Technical bottleneck

Despite significant technological progress, the key challenge remains in the actual implementation process: **insufficient visual perception capabilities in complex farmland environments**. Existing visual solutions generally face the following problems in outdoor scenes:

1. Leaf occlusion and object segmentation problems: In complex environments such as orchards, the occlusion of leaves and branches causes target objects (such as apples) to be segmented into fragmented areas. It is difficult for traditional segmentation algorithms to restore their complete three-dimensional shape, seriously affecting the accuracy of picking path planning.

**2. Point cloud data quality defects**: The point cloud data generated by some solutions has problems such as edge blur, noise interference, and insufficient density, resulting in incomplete capture of the three-dimensional shape and details of the target object, making it difficult to meet picking needs and limiting the robot's operating capabilities.

## Core Breakthrough

Meyer Micro Vision works closely with the Intelligent Equipment Technology Research Center of the Beijing Academy of Agricultural and Forestry Sciences to deeply integrate the **anti-occlusion semantic segmentation algorithm** and the **embedded RGB-D fusion camera** to build three major technical barriers to the visual perception of outdoor picking robots:

**1. Anti-occlusion semantic segmentation algorithm**

Based on the patented algorithm of the Beijing Academy of Agricultural and Forestry Sciences, even when the target is partially obscured by leaves, the system can still accurately restore the complete outline of the apple through **multi-scale feature fusion** and **context-aware model**.

**2. High-density point cloud generation**

M4 Pro can generate high-quality point cloud data in complex outdoor environments, accurately capture the three-dimensional form and details of target objects, and effectively solve the problem of blurred and incomplete data. At the same time, M4 Pro is equipped with a high-resolution RGB camera, which, combined with point cloud data, can provide richer visual information and further improve the recognition accuracy and completeness of target objects.

**3. Client-side real-time data processing**

M4 Pro supports edge computing, and its built-in embedded platform can realize localized operation of algorithms, significantly reducing dependence on the cloud or central processing unit, improving data processing speed and real-time performance, and meeting the needs of high-speed picking scenarios.

## Success Stories

**Intelligent Equipment Technology Research Center of the Beijing Academy of Agricultural and Forestry Sciences** has launched the country's first "harvest-harvest-transport" integrated multi-arm picking robot, which has made breakthroughs in key technologies such as visual recognition of ripe fruits in the natural environment, rigid-flexible coupled picking arm design, and multi-arm collaborative task planning. It has significantly improved the robot's performance in terms of information perception, smooth operation and efficient operation.

In a standard dwarf, densely planted orchard in Changping District, China, the orchard's multi-arm picking robot integrates the **Myer Micro Vision M4 Pro RGB-D camera** to simulate the picking techniques of fruit farmers, gently unscrewing apples and placing them on the conveyor belt, achieving efficient and precise picking operations.
