Foliage occlusion, changing daylight, and different fruit poses make segmentation, 3D localization, and grasp planning difficult in open orchards. This solution combines occlusion-aware semantic segmentation with an RGB-D camera to provide perception data for harvesting robots.

## Scene challenges

- **Foliage occlusion:** Leaves and branches can hide part of a fruit, making it difficult for conventional segmentation to recover the complete contour.
- **Point-cloud quality:** Blurred edges, noise, or low point density reduce the usable 3D shape of the target.
- **On-device processing:** Harvesting tasks need perception and algorithm processing close to the robot to reduce dependence on remote compute.

## Solution components

### Occlusion-aware semantic segmentation

The Intelligent Equipment Technology Research Center of the Beijing Academy of Agricultural and Forestry Sciences uses multi-scale feature fusion and contextual information to help recover apple contours that are partially hidden by foliage.

### RGB-D 3D perception

The M4 Pro provides RGB images and point-cloud data to capture fruit shape and texture for recognition and harvesting-path planning.

### On-device algorithm processing

The M4 Pro supports running algorithms on an embedded platform, allowing data processing at the orchard and enabling the robot to respond within the harvesting workflow.

## Application example

The Intelligent Equipment Technology Research Center of the Beijing Academy of Agricultural and Forestry Sciences developed an integrated multi-arm robot for harvesting, collection, and transport. In a standard dwarf, high-density orchard in Changping, the robot used an MRDVS M4 Pro RGB-D camera to support apple recognition, localization, and harvesting.
