Logistics, manufacturing, and automated lines need fast length, width, height, and volume data. Unlike line-laser profiling, which requires continuous motion, or light-curtain methods that infer volume from occlusion, 3D vision captures the object's surface directly and supports static and multi-target measurement.

## Solution overview

The solution uses a 3D camera to capture target data and calculate length, width, height, and volume. It supports one or multiple targets and can send results to a higher-level system through a communication interface.

## Solution characteristics

- **Millimeter-level measurement:** Historical material reports millimeter-level accuracy. Actual accuracy depends on the camera model, installation, calibration, and target properties.
- **Fast recognition:** Complete target recognition and volume calculation in a short time.
- **Multi-target measurement:** Capture 3D data for multiple objects at the same time.
- **Complex-object adaptation:** Support irregular parts, highly reflective parts, and bulk cargo; in some scenarios, suppress film interference.

## System components

| Component | Function |
| --- | --- |
| M / S Series 3D camera | Capture 3D data and provide camera-side compute. |
| Processing unit | Process 3D data and generate measurement results. |
| Mounting bracket | Keep the camera rigid and stable and reduce installation error. |
| Volume-detection software | Output length, width, height, and volume. |
| Data interface | Send results to the higher-level system. |

## Application scenarios

- **Logistics and warehousing:** Capture load dimensions and volume to support inventory, transport, and loading management.
- **Manufacturing:** Verify product dimensions and collect packaging-process data.
- **Automated lines:** Feed measurement results into existing control workflows.
- **Airport logistics:** Measure baggage or cargo packages to support space matching and sorting.

## Deployment considerations

<Warning>
Dust, oil, and water mist can affect optical imaging. Keep the measurement area and lens clean, and make sure the camera bracket is rigid and stable.
</Warning>

Confirm target types, surface properties, and system-interface requirements before deployment.
