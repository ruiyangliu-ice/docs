With the advancement of lidar technology, laser navigation has now become one of the mainstream navigation methods in the industry, and AMR solutions based on laser SLAM navigation have been implemented in many scenarios.

As the application of laser navigation technology increases, its shortcomings are also exposed, especially in terms of positioning stability, which is difficult to withstand the test of complex scenarios.

**In complex and changeable scenarios**, the location of goods changes at any time, and there are few fixed references. For mobile robots using laser SLAM solutions, frequent entry and exit of warehouses and constant changes in location mean that the laser-scanned contour environment is changing all the time, and it is very difficult to achieve reliable positioning in a constantly changing environment.

**In a large open workshop**, the distance between steel structure columns is large. In this scenario, the mobile robot with laser SLAM navigation will have weakened positioning reliability due to the scarcity of laser scanned point clouds and the lack of effective positioning reference objects.

**Visual SLAM navigation collects surrounding images through the depth camera mounted on the robot and generates dense point cloud images. The collected environmental information is rich enough, and local changes in the environment will not affect the robot's positioning, so it has extremely high scene adaptability.**

## Take the “not easy road”

Since visual SLAM technology has unparalleled advantages over laser SLAM in terms of environmental adaptability, why are there only a handful of robotics companies actually adopting visual SLAM on the market?

**The primary reason that affects the choice of visual SLAM for mobile robots is the high threshold of depth vision technology. **Most AMR companies do not have the conditions to independently develop 3D vision function modules. They have neither a vision R&D team nor relevant technology accumulation.

Although Meyer Micro Vision is a young company, its core technical members have accumulated experience in the fields of optics, computer vision and other fields for nearly two decades. The company has comprehensive R&D capabilities in computer vision + mobile robots.

**Compared with mature lidar, depth vision can be said to be a "not easy road", and Meyer Micro Vision chose the latter.**

After years of technology accumulation and research, Meyer Micro Vision has built a powerful and complete mobile robot depth vision system MRDVS (including self-developed 3D vision sensors and depth vision perception algorithms), becoming the first domestic company to provide an integrated 3D vision software and hardware solution for mobile robots.

By ** equipped with MRDVS, mobile robots can achieve visual positioning, visual obstacle avoidance, and high-precision docking**, and the safety, stability, and intelligence are greatly improved to meet the application needs of more complex scenarios.

**Take the warehousing scene of a large domestic OEM company as an example. **It is understood that the company has an on-site warehouse with more than 3,000 storage spaces. Goods enter and leave the warehouse frequently, and the stacked pallets and goods change at any time, and there is no fixed reference. In addition, the robot may encounter workers and cartons left on the aisles during operation. At the same time, for the sake of the overall beauty of the warehouse and the convenience of deployment, customers do not want to paste QR codes on the ground.

In this case, the company has fewer options, with only laser SLAM and visual SLAM unmanned forklifts available. However, for mobile robots using laser SLAM, it is very difficult to achieve reliable positioning in a constantly changing environment. In the end, the company chose Blue Core Technology’s visual SLAM unmanned forklift solution.

It is understood that **Myer Microvision provides an integrated solution for visual positioning, pallet docking, and visual obstacle avoidance for Blue Core Technology's unmanned forklift.**

**In terms of positioning and navigation**, the forklift adopts a 3D visual SLAM solution. The depth camera is used to collect unmarked three-dimensional environmental information, generate a dense point cloud map, and connect the collected information with the actual position of the robot to complete the robot's autonomous positioning and navigation. Its advantages are: 1. Reliable and stable, not affected by people/vehicles/logistics; 2. Adaptable to large-changing scenes, indoor layout adjustment, cargo placement and movement are not affected; 3. High point arrival accuracy, general environment +/-1cm.

**In terms of pallet docking**, the forklift adopts a 3D visual docking solution. The self-developed docking camera collects images and generates a point cloud image of the pallet, and then combines it with the Blue Core visual perception algorithm to calculate the pallet deviation value, guide the forklift to automatically adjust its posture and fork direction, and successfully fork the pallet.

In addition, ** this forklift also adopts a 3D visual obstacle avoidance solution, ** the obstacle avoidance camera can detect suspended obstacles and low obstacles within the field of view, fully ensuring the safety of the robot's driving.
