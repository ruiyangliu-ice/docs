## Project background

A floor-stacked warehouse operated by a large contract manufacturer has **3,000+** storage locations. Goods and pallets move frequently, leaving few fixed references. The customer also did not want QR codes on the floor. The unmanned forklift must detect low and overhanging obstacles such as people and cartons in the aisles.

## Technology selection

In areas where load positions change frequently or where large open spaces provide few stable features, laser SLAM can be affected by changing contours and insufficient references. The project therefore used a Bluebotics visual-SLAM unmanned forklift integrated with MRDVS visual localization, pallet docking, and 3D visual obstacle avoidance.

## Solution components

| Module | Function |
| --- | --- |
| Visual SLAM localization | A depth camera captures unmarked 3D environmental data for robot localization and navigation. Project records report typical point-arrival accuracy of **±1 cm**. |
| Pallet docking | Calculate pallet deviation from point-cloud data and guide the forklift to adjust pose and fork direction. |
| 3D visual obstacle avoidance | Detect low and overhanging obstacles in the field of view and provide environmental inputs for driving. |

<Info>
Actual localization accuracy and operating performance depend on site structure, environmental changes, installation, and calibration. Validate the system on site before deployment.
</Info>
