With the advancement of Industry 4.0, the demand for intelligent logistics equipment is growing day by day. As a key component of the warehousing and logistics system, intelligent forklifts require a high degree of autonomy and efficient operating capabilities. To achieve this goal, forklifts need to be able to accurately identify and operate pallets to ensure the accuracy and efficiency of material handling.

## Limitations of 2D Vision Technology and LiDAR Identification of Pallets

- Insufficient recognition capabilities in complex environments: Traditional 2D vision systems exhibit low recognition rates and high false alarm rates when identifying different lighting conditions, complex backgrounds, and high-density stacked pallets, making it difficult to cope with pose recognition tasks in three-dimensional space.

- Lack of robustness and flexibility: In practical applications, pallets come in different shapes, colors and materials, which requires the recognition system to have a high degree of adaptability. When dealing with diverse pallets and complex environmental changes, traditional technology often requires a lot of debugging and manual intervention, which increases operation and maintenance costs.

- The problem of balancing positioning accuracy and speed: In industrial applications, pallet recognition not only requires high accuracy, but also needs a fast response speed to adapt to efficient logistics needs. When traditional positioning systems improve accuracy, they usually need to increase the amount of calculations, which affects the real-time performance of the system.

## Solution Overview

Meyer Microvision's pallet recognition solution uses a ToF depth camera and is equipped with a self-developed pallet recognition algorithm. By capturing the three-dimensional point cloud data of the pallet with a depth camera, this solution can accurately identify the geometric features of the pallet, ensuring stable operation even in outdoor environments.

## Core Advantages

- **Accurate positioning, reduced errors**: The solution is developed based on 3D ToF cameras, which can capture the precise position and posture of the pallet in space, and control the distance error within ±10mm within a 2-meter working distance, enabling smart forklifts to achieve high-precision pallet operations in complex warehousing environments, greatly reducing operational errors caused by positioning errors.

- **Adaptive Diversified Pallets**: Combined with Meyer MicroVision’s self-developed algorithm, this solution can identify pallets of different shapes, sizes and materials and make adaptive adjustments without additional manual intervention. This not only improves the versatility of the system, but also reduces identification difficulties caused by differences in pallets, making it more adaptable.

- **Real-time response, improved efficiency**: Through efficient image processing algorithms, the 3D vision system can complete the identification and positioning of pallets in a short time, realizing the entire process of automated operation from initial positioning to precise positioning, greatly improving the operating efficiency of forklifts.
