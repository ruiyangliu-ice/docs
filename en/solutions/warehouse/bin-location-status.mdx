## Background introduction

Challenge: In most warehouses where AGVs have been applied, items are still stacked manually. This will cause the WMS system to be unable to judge the actual occupancy of each warehouse location in real time, resulting in inaccurate information provided by the WMS to the AGVs. If the occupied warehouse cannot be identified in time, the AGV may receive wrong instructions, which not only reduces operating efficiency, but also easily collides with other goods or equipment. In severe cases, it may even cause a safety accident and endanger the safety of personnel and equipment.

Therefore, the warehouse status identification system came into being. Through functions such as automatic positioning, real-time data synchronization, and precise inventory control, the system optimizes the allocation of warehouse resources, reduces human errors, and improves operational efficiency.

**Two common pain points of storage location status recognition sensors:**

- The storage location information transmitted by single-point lidar is not accurate enough and can easily cause stacking accidents.

Some warehouses use single-point lidar for storage location detection, but this ranging radar can only emit one laser beam at a time to form a point on the surface of an object. It may ignore the gaps between cartons or pallets, leading to misidentification of the storage location status, thereby causing stacking accidents.

- The calibration method of pure RGB cameras is single, and there is a risk of misjudgment and missing information.

There are the following disadvantages when using RGB cameras to determine the location status:

- When using deep learning to detect targets, objects outside the training set entering the warehouse may cause false detections and give wrong information.

- Lack of cargo height information makes it difficult to arrange stacking tasks.

- Ultra-wide-angle fisheye cameras have edge distortion problems in warehouse location determination, which affects the accuracy of model training and prediction, and increases server costs.

## Solution Overview

Meyer Micro Vision uses an **RGB-D camera** to provide three-dimensional data and color information of warehouse locations, and automatically identify the presence, placement specifications and abnormal occupancy of goods. It can also distinguish cargo categories through the 3D vision intelligent AI system and output it to the dispatching system to achieve intelligent inbound and outbound inspection. The camera has built-in computing power and does not require an external industrial computer. At the same time, Meyer Micro Vision provides integrated software and hardware solutions for warehouse status identification, as well as professional model training and deployment guidance and other service support.

## Core Advantages

- Comprehensive monitoring: Accurately identify storage location status through three-dimensional data and color information collection, combined with AI technology.

- Simple deployment: The storage location status recognition algorithm is placed on the camera side to reduce deployment and maintenance costs.

- Flexible communication: supports TCP/IP, UDP, HTTP and other communication methods, and reports data to the control system in JSON format in real time.

- Improve efficiency: Real-time transmission of warehouse location information helps the dispatch system allocate tasks quickly and accurately.

## Job description

> **Camera selection suggestions: S10Pro**

> **Camera installation method**

Fixed installation: When the camera is installed on a column or ceiling, it is recommended to install it directly above the storage location to be monitored.

> **Specific workflow**

Step1: The upper-level control system sends task instructions, and the 3D vision camera collects images and accurately obtains the specified warehouse status;

Step 2: The storage location detection results are reported to the upper-layer system through JSON to assist the dispatching system in realizing intelligent inbound and outbound management.

Communication method: TCP, UDP, HTTP

> **Deployment Notes**

Application environment: It is necessary to avoid harsh environments with severe dust and condensation;

Installation requirements: Ensure the rigidity and stability of the camera mounting bracket;
