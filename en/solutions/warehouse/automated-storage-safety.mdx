In automated high-bay warehouses, although steel structure racks appear to be stable, factors such as long-term use and load fluctuations may cause slight geometric deviations. The working height of the stacker can reach 30 meters or even 50 meters. Any deviation may lead to mis-docking or jamming, which may lead to downtime or safety accidents. Therefore, accurately monitoring the position and status of shelves and pallets has become an issue that must be considered to improve warehouse efficiency and safety.

## Solution

Meyer Micro Vision's 3D vision technology accurately locates the three-dimensional spatial data of pallets and shelves through depth perception capabilities, and can monitor the position, shape, offset, tilt, deformation and other states of these elements in real time. Once a problem is discovered, it will be immediately fed back to the stacker control system, which will automatically suspend the operation and alarm to avoid misoperation or potential safety risks.

## Key application scenarios

• Pallet status monitoring: detect the offset, tilt, deformation and other conditions of the pallet to prevent the stacker from hitting the goods.

• Crossbeam deformation monitoring: detect the displacement or deformation of crossbeams or pillars to ensure stable operation of the stacker.

• Cargo space occupancy detection: detect whether there is a cargo loading unit in the cargo space to avoid incorrect delivery operations.
