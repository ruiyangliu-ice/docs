## Background introduction

In modern industrial production and logistics scenarios, automated depalletizing has become a key link to improve efficiency and reduce manual operation risks. Especially in industries involving irregular objects such as soft bags, cartons, and sacks, such as plastic particles, fertilizers, food processing, etc.

> **Industry Challenges in Flexible Packaging Depalletizing**

Irregular objects such as soft bags and cartons are stacked due to their irregular shapes and diverse materials, which increases the difficulty of automatic depalletization. Especially when dealing with deformation, lamination, or diagonal placement, traditional robotic arms are often unable to handle them. The main problems faced by enterprises include:

**• Soft bag deformation**: Soft bags are easily deformed during the stacking process, making it more difficult to grab.

**• Low efficiency**: Manual depalletizing takes a long time, and long-term high-intensity operations are error-prone.

**•Safety hazards**: Pack leaks and broken packets under traditional operations not only increase additional material loss, but also pose safety risks.

## Program Highlights

Facing the above industry challenges, Meyer Microvision's **3D Vision Soft Packaging Depalletizing System** provides accurate visual data and intelligent path planning for depalletizing operations by integrating RGB-D depth vision cameras and intelligent algorithms, significantly improving the efficiency and accuracy of automated depalletizing.

**• High-performance RGB-D camera and AI algorithm**

The camera can capture the surface texture information and point cloud data of the stack at the same time. Combined with the deep learning of the AI ​​algorithm, the system can accurately identify and locate irregular objects such as soft bags and cartons, making full use of their surface texture and edge features to ensure the stability and accuracy of each grabbing action, effectively reducing the phenomenon of dropped and broken packages.

**• Algorithm integrated on the camera side**

The destacking algorithm runs inside the camera, enabling real-time data processing and reducing dependence on external industrial computers. This design greatly simplifies system deployment, reduces overall costs, and improves system response speed and operating efficiency.

**• Strong ability to resist ambient light interference**

The system has excellent resistance to ambient light interference and can maintain stable visual recognition performance in complex lighting environments. Whether it is strong light or low light conditions, the system can effectively capture the details of objects and ensure the smooth execution of depalletizing tasks.

**•Adaptive recognition**

There is no need to collect training data in advance. The system can adaptively identify the stack shapes of soft bags and sacks, and supports complex structures such as six-flowered stacks, five-flowered stacks, and rounded stacks, achieving efficient depalletizing and reducing debugging difficulty.

**• Sequential planning based on soft package location**

By obtaining the location information of each soft package, a reasonable crawling sequence planning is provided. Even in the face of complex stacking forms, the system can ensure that each soft package is processed efficiently and safely.

**• Four-point calibration, simple and efficient**

Meyer Micro Vision provides a four-point calibration method. Users can flexibly select calibration points. The system automatically calculates the conversion matrix between the camera and the robotic arm, which is more convenient than the traditional 9-point or 64-point calibration.
